Thibault Le Tallec

Thibault Le Tallecclose

Young man (18 years old), student at this moment, and a pround passionated personn. I started to be insterested by programming and game development when I was 14 - 15 years old. Now, this is a real passion, and I clearly want to build my life around this... Then, the best way I found to learn and improve is to do projects, again and again. Here's some of my firsts that I like.

Presentation

At that time, I started programming, especially in python, and I set myself the challenge of making a mini game in that language. Knowing that I love RPG type games, I started looking for an interesting concept for this genre. From there, I based myself on the story and concept of an animated manga called DanMachi. The principle is simple: a dungeon, supposed to be infinite, in which we fight to survive, and whose goal is to descend as deep as possible.

To this I could easily add a system of class, statistics, objects or quests...

Realisation

First of all, I had to think about the mechanics I wanted to insert. Let’s start with the class and the statistics: I imagined 5 of them (Knight, Archer, Sword Fighter, Tank and Assasin), each with its own characteristics, strengths and weaknesses etc.

For the combat system, I had little other choice than the turn-by-turn, easier to manage, and having only one terminal interface ...

Then we had to make sure that we were doing things by making limited choices. For this, the game simply asks to the player to enter the number of the desired action.

We can then explore the city and make precisely actions. This gives rise to loops of games, which can be organised into functions.

Finally, how to make feel the progress and the difficulty of the dungeon? ... First, by creating different creatures representing threats, and becoming more and more powerful the more we go deep. We pass from ferocious wolves to dragons in fifty floors ... Then come the rewards: crystals winnable on killed creatures, stored in our inventory, and exchangeable for money. It then allows us to buy various things at the market.

Result

Honestly, this is a game that I’m not very proud of, because it is not at all finished : I had to stop working on it, and it remained in a beta version, playable, but not complete. There are also many bugs to fix, and finally, above all, the interface that makes us play is a bit ridiculous .. given that it is a terminal ...

It was nevertheless a good experience, especially to succeed in organizing a slightly more complex code.
